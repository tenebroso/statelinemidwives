Home Birth

Stateline Midwives provide primary midwifery care for families who choose to have their babies at home. We are licensed, autonomous providers who keep birth safe and revered. We provide evidence-based care, all while having deep respect for you and your baby. Informed choice is important to us, and because of this, all care is done with informed consent and refusal. We support you and your choices. We love to see our growing families thrive.

In a nutshell...

  • Free Consultation
  • Initial visit with full physical and blood work.
  • 24 hour availability for emergencies
  • Comprehensive prenatal care every 4 weeks until 28 weeks of pregnancy, every 2 weeks until 36 weeks and then weekly till you have your baby.
  • Approximately 1 hour prenatal visits tailored to your needs, to build relationships and to clinically assess both you and your baby
  • Nutrition/herbal counseling and Childbirth education
  • Continuous labor support in your home once labor has been established
  • Both land birth and water birth options
  • Newborn exam in your own bed
  • Primary care for both you and baby during your labor, birth and immediate postpartum until the family is comfortable, fed and you are ready to rest.
  • Breastfeeding support
  • Continuous Postpartum support with several postpartum visits for you and baby
  • Contraceptive/family planning education with optional PAP at your six week postpartum visit

Andrea Bukiewicz

Hi! I’m Andrea Bukiewicz. I am a Certified Professional Midwife and a Licensed midwife in the state of Wisconsin. My midwifery journey began in 2007, assisting women in both hospital and home. Here I saw our culture's disillusion towards birth and the harm it can cause. This experience catapulted my midwifery studies, beginning with my midwifery degree from National College of Midwifery in Taos, New Mexico. I have had the honor of participating in over 900 births, the bulk of them in the homes of my clients. I believe in the power of women mentoring women. This belief not only held true during my 4 year traditional midwifery apprenticeship, but it also reflects in the care that I give. 

In 2013 my studies sent me to Bali, Indonesia. Here I spent a summer interning at Bumi Sehat Birth Center and apprenticed under awarded humanitarian, Ibu Robin Lim. This experience helped me mature into the midwife you will know today. A strong woman who is here to protect our rite of passage and help women birth in such a way that honors its process, all while providing evidence based care and informed consent. 

My own introduction to the healing arts and feminine teaching looked very similar to my midwifery apprenticeships, my grandmother sharing her eucalyptus remedies and family

stories. Her love began the preparation for what was to come and helped cultivate my spirit and trade. Outside of midwifery and keeper of women's mysteries, I am an athlete, ballerina, herbalist, writer, sex educator, devout animist, student, and avid traveler with my two children and our Goldendoodle, Loki.

Melissa Kane

I am Melissa Kane, and I use she/they pronouns. I have been working as a birth worker since 2012. After a positive experience with a doula during the birth of my third child, I pursued doula training. After a few years of working in our community, I realized I truly loved my work and I wanted to shift my focus and role to become a primary care provider. I observed many people giving birth in environments where providers distrusted them and the birthing process, leading to traumatic outcomes for parents and babies in disproportionate numbers. I do not want to be a part of that system. I want to support people in finding their voice, strength, and power during pregnancy, labor, and birth. Becoming a CPM. allows me to participate in birth with education and safety while honoring the natural process that has been bringing souls into the world for centuries. I am passionate about offering evidence-based and culturally competent care to all my clients.

In addition, I am a Master Reiki Practitioner. When I'm not supporting clients, I can usually be found at home on our small farm in Kenosha County, where we have a few animals, gardens, and a tiny orchard. As a farmer and chef, I continuously learn and grow in the farm-to-table community. I am also an avid swimmer in both the pool and open water. I enjoy reading, listening to podcasts, camping, and meditating on the front porch.
